An Analysis of Canadian Advertisement Trends

by Ella Hugie


Welcome to My Project Website

This website showcases the findings of my project on Canadian Advertising Expenditure. Utilizing data from the Government of Canada Open Portal covering the period from 2018 to 2023, the study delves into how digitization has influenced advertising trends throughout Canada. By analyzing expenditure patterns, this project seeks to quantify the changing dynamics of advertising within the country.

Here are some interactive visualizations highlighting the main findings of my analysis.

Interactive Visualization 1: Total Ad Spending Over the Years

This graph illustrates the steady increase in advertising expenditure over the years, reflecting the growing importance of advertising in our economy. While there is a notable dip in total spending during 2020, likely due to the Covid-19 pandemic, there is overall an upward trend indicating the continual growth of advertising efforts in Canada.

Interactive Visualization 2: Ad Spending by Region

This bar chart offers a view of advertising expenditure across different regions. The data reveals a notable trend: the majority of spending is overwhelmingly concentrated in the “National” category, followed by “Ontario” and “Multi-Market”. This distribution underscores the dominance of national advertising in budget allocation, prompting further exploration into the specific nature of national advertisements.

Interactive Visualization 3: Ad Spending by Media Type

The bar chart illustrates the distribution of advertising expenditure across various media types. It reveals that the highest spending is allocated to “Digital” media, followed by “Linear Television” and “Out of Home” media. This distribution emphasizes a significant shift towards digitization in advertising practices, with digital media emerging as the primary channel for marketing efforts.

Interactive Visualization 4: Spending over Time by Media Type

This visualization depicts advertising expenditure trends across various media types over time. Notably, spending on “Digital” and “Linear Television” is on the rise, while other media types show a decline. This shift reflects the changing landscape of advertising, emphasizing the increasing dominance of digital platforms and television.

Interactive Visualization 5: Percentage Spending by Market Size for Various Media Types

This plot illustrates the evolving trends in media spending concerning market size index. Digital spending demonstrates a notable increase as market size grows, contrasting with declining trends observed in newspapers and radio. Particularly noteworthy is the higher proportion of radio spending in smaller markets. These insights underscore the shifting landscape of advertising preferences, emphasizing the growing significance of digital platforms while highlighting nuanced dynamics across market sizes.
